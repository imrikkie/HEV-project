Topic Name- Hybrid Vehicle with Smooth Mechanical-Electric Propulsion Transition

The core premise of this problem statement is to leverage the existing CVs, which are already equipped with internal combustion engines, and adapt them to serve as supplementary power sources for EVs. By coupling the versatility of CVs with the eco-friendliness of EVs, this approach aims to extend the driving range of EVs and reduce their dependence on extensive charging infrastructure. To achieve this integration, complex engineering challenges must be addressed, including the development of efficient power management systems, mechanical adaptations, and the integration of CVs within EV platforms. Additionally, the system should adhere to safety, emissions, and regulatory standards while optimizing overall vehicle performance. Technique - The fuel is used only when the main battery level is below a certain point and isnot able to keep up with the vehicle’s requirement. Then is the internal combustionengine. This design has gases infused in either the admission complex also called as the burning region, here it combines with air, and the air/fuel blend which is ignited from the flash of a spark plug. Following this is the onboard charger which processes the approaching alternating current provided by means of the charging point and changes to direct current power for charging the main battery. It screens battery attributes, for example, V, I, temp, and the remaining percentage of charging. Then is the power electronics controller. These units deal with the progression of energy conveyed by the main battery, commanding the rpm of the main engine, and the torque produced by it. Team Name: Mechanites
